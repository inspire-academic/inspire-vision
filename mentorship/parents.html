How We Keep Your Child Safe
Safeguarding isn't an afterthought — it shapes how mentors are approved and how every pairing is made.

Every mentor is reviewed before approval. Nobody is matched with a young person on the basis of an application alone — our team reviews each one individually, a process that may include a background check.

Pairings are made by our team, not chosen by mentors or mentees. There is no self-service matching anywhere in the platform — a mentor can never assign themselves to a young person.

Your child can always ask for help. A private "Ask for Help" and "Prayer & Support" channel lets them reach out at any time, and every request is reviewed by our team.

Parents and guardians may be involved in their child's mentorship journey, particularly for mentees under 18 — this is part of what your child agrees to when they enroll.

Common Questions

Who are the mentors?

Adults who apply through our mentor application, share their motivation for mentoring, and go through a safeguarding review before they're ever approved to be matched with a young person.

How is my child matched with a mentor?

Our team makes every pairing directly — your child doesn't browse or choose a mentor, and a mentor can't select a mentee. It's a deliberate, one-to-one match made on your child's behalf.

Can I stay involved as a parent or guardian?

Yes. Parents and guardians may be involved in the mentorship journey, especially for mentees under 18 — this is agreed to as part of enrollment.

What if I have a concern?

Your child can raise anything, anytime, through the private Ask for Help / Prayer & Support channel in their dashboard, which our team reviews directly.

Is this program faith-based?

Yes — Inspire Mentorship is rooted in faith and character formation alongside practical goals and life skills, as part of the wider Inspire Vision movement.
