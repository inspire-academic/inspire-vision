Resources

🤝 Starting Well
Your first couple of sessions set the tone. Lead with curiosity, not an agenda — ask about their week, what they're proud of, what's been hard. Let silence sit; young people often need a moment before they open up. You don't need to have answers ready. Being consistently present matters more than being wise.
👂 Signs a Mentee Might Need Extra Support
Watch for a sudden drop in check-in mood over several days, a mentee going quiet after being talkative, or mentions of things they're struggling with at home or school that sound heavier than usual. You don't have to solve it — your job is to notice, listen, and encourage them toward Prayer & Support or a trusted adult. If you ever believe a mentee is in immediate danger, contact our team straight away rather than waiting for your next session.
💬 Conversation Starters
"What's one thing you're proud of from this week?" · "What's been the hardest part of your week?" · "If this month went perfectly, what would be different?" · "What's something you believe about yourself that you wish you didn't?" · "Who's someone you look up to, and why?"
🛡️ Safeguarding Reminders
Keep all communication with mentees on platform channels, not personal phone numbers or social media. Session Notes are private to you — use them to track anything worth remembering or flagging, not as a diary. If a mentee discloses something concerning, don't promise secrecy — tell them gently that you may need to loop in our team to keep them safe, then do so.
📈 Using Session Notes Well
A quick note after each session — even two lines — makes the next one better. What did you talk about? What should you follow up on? Anything you want to remember about how they're doing? Future you (and your mentee) will be glad you wrote it down.
